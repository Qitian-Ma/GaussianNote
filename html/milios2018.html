Summary

From Bayesian point of view, the parameter of a Multinomial distribution \(Multi(\pi)\) can be generated by a Dirichlet distribution \(Dir(\alpha)\), which can be decomposed by C independent Gamma Distribution \(\Gamma(\alpha_i, 1)\). The independence ensure the possibility of dividing multi-output classification problem into C independent binary classification problem. In order for the noise to be normal, Gamma distribution can be further approximated by log Normal distribution by second-moment matching. \(\tilde{x_i} âˆ¼ lnN(\tilde{y_i}, \tilde{\sigma}_i^2)\)

Computationally speaking, \(\tilde{y}_i = log \alpha_i - \tilde{\sigma}_i^2 / 2\), \(\tilde{\sigma}_i^2 = log(1 / \alpha_i + 1)\)
